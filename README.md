# Gold & Silver Market Trends Analysis (2000 - 2026) 📈📊

## Project Overview
This project delivers an end-to-end Data Engineering and Analytics solution designed to analyze and visualize historical Gold and Silver market trends from 2000 to 2026. By building clean data pipelines and modeling the dataset, this project uncovers critical macroeconomic insights, such as the safe-haven effect during financial crises, the volatility of speculative bubbles, and the impact of banking failures on asset liquidity.

## Tech Stack & Architecture
- **Data Modeling & Storage:** SQL Server (3NF Normalization, Indexing, and Schema Architecture)
- **ETL & Data Cleaning:** Python (Pandas)
- **Data Visualization & Analytics:** Power BI (Dual-Axis Charts, Interactive Matrix Reports, and Dynamic Tooltips)

---

## Key Macroeconomic Insights Uncovered

### 1. The 2008 Financial Crisis & The Safe Haven Effect
Following the 2008 global economic crash, Gold Close prices experienced a massive, sustained rally lasting until 2012. Trading Volumes (`avg_volume`) for Gold skyrocketed during this exact period, confirming a healthy, liquidity-driven bull market as institutional capital fled to safety.

### 2. The 2011 Silver Bubble
While Gold climbed steadily, Silver witnessed a historic, parabolic spike in 2011 driven by heavy market speculation. However, volume patterns show this liquidity quickly dried up, leading to a sharp collapse. While Gold maintained its baseline value, Silver took nearly a decade to recover, demonstrating high asset volatility.

### 3. The Pandemic Shock & Bank Failures (2020 - 2023)
The post-COVID-19 landscape, paired with global inflation and high-profile banking collapses up to 2023 (e.g., SVB, Credit Suisse), triggered systemic fear. As trust in fiat currencies dropped, capital fled to hard assets, injecting massive trading volume into precious metals and initiating the current super-cycle.

### 4. 2025 - 2026 Record Highs
Driven by cumulative global inflation, currency devaluation, and ongoing geopolitical tensions, Gold smashed through all historical resistance levels in 2025 and 2026, reaching its highest aggregate price levels on record.
